Why I Left Instagram

  1. Friends, family, acquaintences, enemies, brands, pop, porn, chips and dip, games, Russian bots, trolls. There is no distinction here. No demarcation. All are subsumed into advertising.
  2. You draw, you write, you share whatever it is you do, but none of it is for you. None of it is for your audience either. Every action is mediated. It's engagement, twice removed.
  3. 'Being There' is an expansive idea. It relates to everyone you love and everything you entertain. To pursue this on a platform is to approach all worth with a sanitary yardstick.
  4. While 'Feel Good' is argued to be "too easy", more often than not, the real issue is this: ubiquitousness. It sullies the wrinkles of worth in all activity, empathy included.
  5. It makes you make lists on 'why you feel like shit'. Yeesh!
